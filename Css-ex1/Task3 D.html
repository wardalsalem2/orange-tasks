In today's rapidly changing world, technology plays an indispensable role in almost every aspect of our lives. From the moment we wake up to the time we go to bed, we are surrounded by various forms of technology. Smartphones, computers, and social media have all become integral parts of daily existence, helping us stay connected with friends and family, access information instantly, and perform tasks more efficiently. However, while these advancements have undoubtedly brought about numerous benefits, they also come with their own set of challenges. The overuse of technology has led to concerns about privacy, mental health, and the impact on social relationships. People spend more time in front of screens than interacting with others face-to-face, which can result in a sense of isolation despite being more "connected" than ever before. Additionally, the constant flow of information can sometimes overwhelm individuals, leading to stress and a lack of focus. As we continue to rely on technology, it is essential to find a balance that allows us to take advantage of its many benefits while also safeguarding our well-being and maintaining meaningful human connections.

Furthermore, a significant portion of academic research and literature is published in English, giving those who are proficient in the language an edge in the academic and professional fields. In addition, English is the dominant language on the internet, with the majority of online content being written in English. This opens up a wealth of information a nd opportunities for those who can understand and communicate in the language. Learning English not only allows individuals to access these opportunities, but it also fosters personal growth, confidence, and a deeper understanding of the world around them.
